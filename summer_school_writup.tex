\section{Introduction}

We will be modeling 1D radiative transfer by performing Monte Carlo sampling of
the underlying physical distributions.  This method differs from Implict Monte
Carlo by treating the physical process on an event-by-event basis.  As each
event happens, the underlying probabilities of each event occuring change,
because of the nonlinear relation between opacities $\sigma$, temperature $T$, and the
Planckian emission source $B(\nu,T)$ (which is proportional to temperature to
the fourth power).  This non-linearity makes this a difficult problem to
parallel, and the non-linearity of the sampled probability distributions results
in physics that are difficult to resolve.
